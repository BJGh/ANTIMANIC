What is Tonsillitis
Tonsillitis is an inflammation of the tonsils, which are two lymph nodes located on each side of the back of your throat. It can be caused by viral or bacterial infections, and it often leads to symptoms such as:



Sore throat: This is usually the most prominent symptom.

Swollen tonsils: They may appear red and swollen, and you might see white or yellow patches on them.

Difficulty swallowing: Pain can make it hard to swallow food or liquids.

Fever: You may experience a high temperature.

Bad breath: This can occur due to the infection.

Ear pain: Sometimes, the pain can radiate to the ears.


Tonsillitis can be acute (short-term) or chronic (long-lasting). Treatment often depends on the cause; viral tonsillitis usually resolves on its own, while bacterial tonsillitis may require antibiotics. If you have any specific concerns or symptoms, feel free to ask! 😊
